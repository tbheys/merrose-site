Case studies

Volkswagen

I joined the VW team a week after the “Dieselgate” scandal broke. For 2 and a half years, I was the digital voice of VW, and gosh it that fun.

I also managed a few other things like redesigning the way they presented their cars to the world, enabling a new UX and IA design that's still used today, and transforming their website into trend-setting morsels of information with stunning photography, crafting individually branded personas for each vehicle--all work that was ardently grounded in the legendary and iconic VW voice.

I can't pick a singular case study. I can only say that we made it through Dieselgate, turned things around (to the tune of a 600% stock increase in the following 2 years I was there) and stayed true to who we were.

Sprint

Sprint was one of my first traditional advertising clients. We put out tons of work together during an interesting inflection point for the brand. I wrote so many radio spots that I memorized the speed-read disclaimers. We also did other fun stuff over the years including some witty Windy City bus wraps that turned the average Chicagoans commute into a metaphor about cell coverage. A full brand-takeover of Arrowhead Stadium, home of NFL’s Kansas City Chiefs. And some national TV spots in multiple languages for all North American markets.
